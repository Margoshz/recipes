Pampushki with garlic

back Borscht Pampushki with garlic Cutlet Kiev

Description

Fluffy, soft, aromatic, airy - the best substitute for bread! Ukrainian pampushki with garlic are baked in the oven and traditionally served with borscht. The dough for them is kneaded with yeast and water with the addition of milk. This is one of the best types of savory pastries!

Ingredients

  • 400-450 g Wheat flour
  • 250 ml Milk
  • 100 g Butter (+for lubricating the mold)
  • 10 g Dry yeast
  • 1 tbsp. Sugar
  • ½ h.p. Salt
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic
  • 5-6 twigs of dill
  • 2 tbsp. Sunflower oil
  • 1 tbsp. Negar
  • to taste salt and pepper

How to Prepare

  1. Prepare the ingredients for the dough: 400-450 g of wheat flour, 250 ml of milk, 100 g of softened butter, 10 g of dry yeast, 1 tbsp sugar and ½ tsp salt.
  2. Mix the dry ingredients in a bowl first - flour, yeast, sugar and salt, mix. Then pour in warm milk and add butter. The oil can be replaced with sunflower oil, then you will get lean pampushki.
  3. Knead the elastic dough, it should not stick to your hands. Leave the dough in a warm place for 40-45 minutes, covered with a towel or film. Wrap the dough and form 12 pampushki.
  4. Carefully lay out the pampushki in a form pre-greased with sunflower or butter. Leave the pampushki in the form for another 30 minutes.
  5. Bake the pampushki in an oven heated to 180 degrees for 25-30 minutes.
  6. For the filling, peel 2-3 cloves of garlic and chop very finely with a knife.
  7. Wash and dry 5-6 sprigs of dill and chop finely. Combine with garlic. Add 2 tbsp. of sunflower oil, 1 tbsp. of vinegar and salt with pepper to taste.
  8. Generously pour the garlic dressing of the pampushki while they are still hot, so they will be better sob with aromas.
    9. Cooking is easy!
